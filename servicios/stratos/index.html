Who it's for

Weather impacts almost everything that we do and every product we touch, from the clothes that we choose to wear to the products that appear on local store shelves. To say that the accuracy of forecasts is simply important is an understatement. Stratos delivers truly global weather data with regularity, and with enough data points to remove random errors that are seen today in the form of lost lives, lost dollars, and general uncertainty. We're rapidly approaching a time when getting caught off guard by the weather is no longer an option.

02

What it does

Improving Forecasts
With Better Data

Forecasting models are made up of complex formulas that process several kinds of weather data into an easy-to-read format (e.g. tomorrow will be sunny with highs in the low 70s). Unfortunately, the models continue to improve while the underlying data is becoming increasingly erratic — there simply isn't enough of it, the data — and it has become obvious that advancements in formulas alone will not improve our holistic understanding of the weather. This year, Stratos will provide ten times more GPS-RO based weather data than the sum of all publicly funded weather satellites and usher in an incredible step forward in making weather forecasts as predictable as the sunrise is each morning.

How it works

Repurposing A Public Good

GPS satellites provide immense value to society and have made it possible to replace paper maps with your mobile phone. These same satellites are used by Spire to measure global weather patterns. Spire satellites, which orbit close to Earth's atmosphere, listen for GPS satellite signals - which are impacted as they pass through Earth's atmosphere. Using a process called GPS radio occultation, Spire measures the change in GPS signal readings to calculate very precise profiles for temperature, pressure, and humidity here on Earth.
