02/ MARCH 2015

DARPA releases concept footage of launch vehicle - 100lb payload for <$1M

Since 2011, The Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency – the U.S. military’s organization focused on seeding and developing new technology concepts – has been developing a launch vehicle that can carry up to 100lbs of payload to orbit for $1M, and can be deployed in less than 24 hours.

DARPA has deemed this the Airborne Launch Assist Space Access program, or ALASA, and has awarded a contract to Boeing to be its prime contractor. The launch vehicle will carry one or multiple small satellites, and be launched from an F-15 fighter jet. At a certain altitude, the launch vehicle would separate from the airplane and its own engines would fire, launching it to space. You can view DARPA’s incredible video rendering of this in action on the DARPA website.

The ability to “launch on demand” with technology such as ALASA will offer small satellite operators unprecedented flexibility in terms of schedule and orbital placement. With the recent announcements by both Google and OneWeb of plans for mega-constellations of small satellites, as well as the rise of a host of other small satellite start ups, demand for this flexibility will continue to grow. In addition to DARPA, both established and start-up launch companies are streaming into this market and offering dedicated launch services for small satellites.

DARPA’s efforts with the ALASA program signify that the US government is serious about small satellites, and that in the future there will be demand from government customers for dedicated small satellite launches and small satellite capabilities. DARPA plans to conduct the first orbital test of ALASA in early 2016, with 11 more demonstrations scheduled throughout 2016.

The Bottom Line:

Dedicated launch vehicles for small satellites – emanating from both government and the private sector – are critical enabling technologies for improving access to space. Having a plethora of launch options will allow operators of small satellites to maximize their value to their customers and to society.
